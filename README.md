# WTI-Crude-Oil-Options-Trading-Strategy-A-GARCH-Guided-ATM-Straddle-Strategy

  First off, I want to acknowledge my friends and classmates Hunter Gotwalt, Daniel Alexander, Garrett Brady, and Declan Campbell for their amazing effort, collaboration, and hardwork. We worked together to research the problem, develop our approach, and execute the analysis from start to finish.
  
  Commodities are the natural resources that drive the world’s economy. They are among the most intriguing and complex asset classes to analyze because of the various factors, both economic and non-economic, that must be taken into consideration when working with these assets. Currently, amid the global geopolitical landscape, oil has been a major talking point in headlines and the broader macroeconomic landscape. As current QCF students at Georgia Tech, our interest in this space and the rigorous combination of the dynamic, ever-changing landscape were major motivations for this project. Additionally, the various factors that go into accurately analyzing this thought-provoking industry further piqued our interest, along with the mathematical complexity of derivatives. Specifically, we developed an options trading strategy based on the difference between realized and implied volatility. Since we know oil is a highly volatile asset, we used a volatility strategy, such as a straddle, to capture the difference between realized and implied volatility, to maximize returns.
