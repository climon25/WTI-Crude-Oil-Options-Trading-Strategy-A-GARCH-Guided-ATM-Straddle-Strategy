# WTI-Crude-Oil-Options-Trading-Strategy-A-GARCH-Guided-ATM-Straddle-Strategy

  Commodities are the natural resources that drive the world’s economy. With that said, they are among the most intriguing and complex asset classes to analyze because of the various factors, both economic and non-economic, that must be taken into consideration when working with these assets. Currently, amid the global geopolitical landscape, oil has been a major talking point in headlines and the broader macroeconomic landscape. As current QCF students, our interest in this space and the rigorous combination of the dynamic, ever-changing landscape were major motivations for this project. Additionally, the various factors that go into accurately analyzing this thought-provoking industry further piqued our interest, along with the mathematical complexity of derivatives. Specifically, we are developing an options trading strategy based on the difference between realized and implied volatility, which will be explained in further detail later. Since we know oil is a highly volatile asset, we will use a volatility strategy, such as a straddle, to capture the difference between realized and implied volatility, so we can do what any aspiring trader wants to do: make enormous returns.
